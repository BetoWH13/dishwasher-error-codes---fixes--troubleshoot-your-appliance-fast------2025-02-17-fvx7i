Samsung Dishwasher Issues – Common Problems & How to Fix Them

Introduction

Samsung dishwashers are packed with advanced features, but like any appliance, they can run into issues. If your Samsung dishwasher isn't working properly, don't worry—we've got you covered. This guide will help you troubleshoot the most common Samsung dishwasher problems and how to fix them. For a complete list of Samsung error codes, check out our Samsung Dishwasher Error Codes guide.

1️⃣ Samsung Dishwasher Not Draining

Symptoms: Dishwasher finishes the cycle but water remains at the bottom.

For more detailed guidance on drainage issues, visit our comprehensive guide on How to Fix a Dishwasher That Won't Drain.

  • ✅ Clogged Drain Filter – Remove and clean the filter at the bottom of the dishwasher.
  • ✅ Blocked Drain Hose – Check for kinks or clogs in the hose; clear any debris.
  • ✅ Faulty Drain Pump – If you hear clicking sounds, the pump may need replacement.
  • ✅ Garbage Disposal Connection Issue – Ensure the knockout plug inside the disposal inlet has been removed.

2️⃣ Samsung Dishwasher Beeping & Flashing Lights

Symptoms: The dishwasher beeps continuously or flashes error lights.

  • ✅ LC Error (Leak Detection) – Check for leaks and make sure the base of the dishwasher is dry.
  • ✅ 4C Error (Water Supply Issue) – Ensure the water inlet valve is functioning and not clogged.
  • ✅ 5C Error (Drainage Problem) – Clean the filter and check the drain hose.
  • ✅ 7E Error (Waterwall Spray Arm Malfunction) – Ensure the spray arm can move freely.

3️⃣ Samsung Dishwasher Not Cleaning Dishes Properly

Symptoms: Dishes come out dirty or with residue.

  • ✅ Clogged Spray Arms – Remove spray arms and clean them to ensure proper water flow.
  • ✅ Low Water Temperature – Run hot water in the sink before starting the dishwasher.
  • ✅ Too Much or Wrong Detergent – Use a detergent specifically designed for dishwashers.
  • ✅ Overloading or Poor Loading – Ensure dishes are loaded properly for optimal cleaning.

4️⃣ Samsung Dishwasher Won't Start

Symptoms: Pressing the start button does nothing.

  • ✅ Child Lock is Enabled – Disable child lock mode by holding the relevant button for 3 seconds.
  • ✅ Door Not Fully Closed – Ensure the door is latched properly.
  • ✅ Tripped Circuit Breaker – Check the home circuit breaker and reset if needed.
  • ✅ Faulty Control Panel – Try resetting the dishwasher by unplugging it for a few minutes.

5️⃣ Samsung Dishwasher Leaking Water

Symptoms: Water pooling under the dishwasher.

  • ✅ Loose or Damaged Door Gasket – Inspect and replace the door seal if it's worn out.
  • ✅ Overloading the Dishwasher – Too many dishes can cause water to escape.
  • ✅ Using Too Much Detergent – Excess suds can push water out of the unit.
  • ✅ Cracked Water Inlet Valve – Inspect and replace if leaking.

Conclusion

Most Samsung dishwasher problems can be fixed with basic troubleshooting, saving you from costly repairs. If you're still having trouble, a detailed repair manual can help you diagnose and fix the issue.
